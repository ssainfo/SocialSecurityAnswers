Medicare Basics Guide

Medicare is health insurance for people 65+, certain disabled individuals, or those with ESRD. Here’s what you need to know in 2025:

Medicare Parts Explained

  • Part A (Hospital Insurance): Covers inpatient hospital stays, skilled nursing, hospice. Free if you worked 10+ years.
  • Part B (Medical Insurance): Doctor visits, outpatient care, preventive services. 2025 premium: $174.70/month (higher for high earners).
  • Part C (Medicare Advantage): Private plans combining A, B, often D. See below for details.
  • Part D (Drug Coverage): Prescription drugs via private plans. Costs vary.

When to Enroll

Initial Enrollment Period (IEP): 7 months around your 65th birthday (3 before, month of, 3 after). Miss it, and you may face penalties.

Special Enrollment Period (SEP): If working past 65 with employer coverage, enroll later without penalty.

General Enrollment: Jan 1–Mar 31 yearly, but penalties apply.

How to Apply

  • Online: Via SSA.gov 3 months before 65. Apply.
  • Phone: 1-800-772-1213 (7 AM–7 PM, Mon–Fri).

Medicare Advantage Plans

Medicare Advantage (Part C) offers an alternative to Original Medicare, often including Part D and extras like vision or dental. Plans vary by region and cost.

  • Pros: All-in-one coverage, lower out-of-pocket costs.
  • Cons: Limited networks, prior authorization needed.
  • Find Plans: Use Medicare’s Plan Finder. Explore Medicare Advantage Plans.

Extra Help Program

Low-income? Extra Help reduces Part D costs (premiums, deductibles). Apply at SSA.gov.
