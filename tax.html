Social Security Tax Basics Guide

Some Social Security benefits may be taxable depending on your income. Here’s a simple breakdown:

  • Taxable Amount: 50-85% of benefits may be taxed if your combined income exceeds $25,000 (single) or $32,000 (married filing jointly).
  • How to Check: Use IRS tools or consult a tax professional. Tax Info.
  • Filing: Report benefits on your tax return using Form SSA-1099.

Example: Mike, single, gets $18,000/year from Social Security and $10,000 from a part-time job. His combined income ($28,000) exceeds $25,000, so 50% of his benefits ($9,000) is taxable.
