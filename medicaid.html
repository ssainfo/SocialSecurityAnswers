Medicaid Info Guide

Medicaid is a federal-state program for low-income individuals, covering medical and long-term care. Here’s the 2025 rundown:

Who Qualifies?

  • Low-Income: Varies by state; often tied to Federal Poverty Level (FPL).
  • Groups: Children, pregnant women, elderly, disabled, some parents.
  • SSI Link: In most states, SSI recipients auto-qualify.

Example: Sarah, on SSI with $943/month, gets Medicaid automatically in her state.

What’s Covered?

Doctor visits, hospital care, prescriptions, and long-term care (nursing homes, home care)—unlike Medicare. Coverage varies by state.

Example: Tom, 70, uses Medicaid to cover nursing home costs after exhausting savings.

Medicaid & Medicare

Dual eligible? Medicaid can pay Medicare premiums and copays. Apply separately if not on SSI.

Example: Jane, 65, gets Medicare Part B ($174.70/month) paid by Medicaid due to low income.

How to Apply

  • State Agency: Contact your state’s Medicaid office or use HealthCare.gov.
  • SSI Path: SSI approval often triggers Medicaid in 35 states.
  • Find Help: State Contacts.
