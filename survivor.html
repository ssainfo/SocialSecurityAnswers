Survivor Benefits Guide

Financial support for spouses, children, and dependent parents after a worker who paid Social Security taxes passes away. Clear rules, real examples, and exact steps to apply.

👥 Who Can Get Survivor Benefits?

Widows / Widowers

Age 60+ (or 50+ if disabled), married at least 9 months, or caring for a child under 16.

Divorced Spouses

Married 10+ years, unmarried, age 60+ (or 50+ if disabled).

Children

Under 18 (or 19 if in high school), or disabled before age 22 and unmarried.

Dependent Parents

Age 62+, received at least 50% of support from the deceased worker.

REAL EXAMPLE

Lisa’s husband died at 55 after 20 years of work. She is 61 and receives 100% of his benefit ($2,000/month) at her Full Retirement Age. Their 15-year-old son receives 75% ($1,500/month) until he turns 18.

💰 How Much Can You Get?

  • Widow(er) at Full Retirement Age Up to 100%
  • Widow(er) age 60–66 71.5% – 99% (rises each year)
  • Child or caring spouse 75% of the deceased’s benefit
  • One dependent parent 82.5%
  • Two dependent parents 75% each

ANOTHER EXAMPLE

Tom dies at 62 with a $2,400 monthly benefit. His 60-year-old widow gets $1,716 (71.5%) today and will receive the full $2,400 at age 67. Their disabled daughter (age 25) receives $1,800 (75%) for life.

Pro tip: SSA pays only the higher of survivor or your own retirement/disability benefit — never both.

Auxiliary Benefit: Lump-Sum Death Payment

One-time $255 payment to help with funeral costs.

💍

Spouse

Living with the worker or already eligible for benefits on their record.

🧒

Child

Eligible if no qualifying spouse exists.

⏰

Deadline

Apply within 2 years of death.

How to Report a Death

  • 🏠
    Funeral home almost always reports it automatically using the SSN.
  • 📞
    You can call 1-800-772-1213 (TTY 1-800-325-0778)
    Mon–Fri, 8 AM–7 PM ET
  • 📄
    Death certificate helps but is not required to begin the process.

How to Apply for Survivor Benefits

You cannot apply online — you must contact SSA directly:

1-800-772-1213 (TTY 1-800-325-0778)
Visit your local SSA office (use the SSA Locator)
Documents needed: SSN, death certificate, marriage/birth certificates, deceased’s most recent tax return.
START YOUR APPLICATION TODAY

After You’re Approved — Report These Changes

Marriage or remarriage (before age 60)
Work & earnings (limits apply before FRA)
Change of address, school status, or disability improvement

Call 1-800-772-1213 or submit Form SSA-795 online. Reporting prevents overpayments and penalties.
