How to Replace Your Social Security Card in 2025

Lost or damaged your Social Security card? Whether it’s for you or your child, here’s how to get a replacement quickly and easily in 2025. Replacing a card is free, but you may not need a physical card—knowing your Social Security number (SSN) is often enough.

Replacement Process for Adults

You can replace your card online, by mail, or in person. Here’s the step-by-step process:

Step 1: Check Eligibility

For online replacement, you need a My SSA account, be 18+, have a U.S. mailing address, and a state-issued ID (most states qualify).

Step 2: Apply Online or In-Person

Use My SSA online, or complete Form SS-5 and submit by mail or at an office (appointment required—see below).

Step 3: Receive Your Card

Mailed in 10-14 business days to your U.S. address. Call 1-800-772-1213 if delayed beyond 14 days.

Example: John, 35, loses his card. He logs into My SSA, requests a replacement with his driver’s license, and gets a new card in 12 days.

Replacement Process for Children

Parents or guardians must apply for kids under 18. Online isn’t an option—you’ll need to go in person or mail Form SS-5:

  • Step 1: Gather documents (see below).
  • Step 2: Complete Form SS-5 (download here).
  • Step 3: Mail or visit an office with an appointment (call your local office).
  • Step 4: Card arrives in 10-14 business days.

Example: Maria replaces her 10-year-old’s card. She mails Form SS-5 with his birth certificate, his school ID, and her driver’s license, and the new card arrives in 11 days.

Documents Needed

All documents must be originals or certified copies—no photocopies or notarized versions. Here’s what you need:

  • For Adults:
    • Identity: U.S. driver’s license, state ID, or passport.
    • Citizenship: U.S. birth certificate or passport (if not online).
    • Name Change (if applicable): Marriage certificate, divorce decree, or court order.
  • For Children:
    • Child’s Identity: School ID, medical record, or adoption decree (birth certificate alone isn’t enough).
    • Child’s Citizenship: U.S. birth certificate or passport.
    • Your Identity: Parent/guardian’s driver’s license, state ID, or passport.

Learn More: See the full list of acceptable documents at SSA’s Document Guide.

Appointment Requirement (New in 2025)

As of January 6, 2025, the SSA highly recommends scheduling an appointment for in-person visits, including card replacements. Walk-ins are still accepted but may face longer waits. To book:

  • Call your local SSA office at the number listed (Mon–Fri, 8 AM–7 PM).
  • Or use the online scheduler after starting your application at My SSA.

Example: Tom needs a new card but can’t apply online. He calls his local office, books an appointment for next week, and brings his ID.

Tips and Updates

  • Free Service: There’s no fee for a replacement card (up to 10 lifetime, 3 per year).
  • 2025 Update: Online applications now include self-scheduling for office visits if needed, saving time.
  • Fraud Alert: Beware of scams—SSA never charges for cards or asks for payment over the phone. Report to Fraud Page.
