How to Replace Your Social Security Card in 2025

Lost, damaged, or stolen your Social Security card? Replacing it in 2025 is straightforward and free—whether it’s for yourself or your child. You don’t always need a physical card (your SSN often suffices), but if you do, this guide covers everything: steps for adults and kids, documents, appointment updates, and tips to avoid delays or scams. Let’s get started!

Why Replace Your Card?

A Social Security card proves your SSN for jobs, taxes, or benefits. While you can memorize your number, some situations (e.g., new employment, applying for SSI) may require the physical card. In 2025, the SSA offers online, mail, and in-person options—each with specific requirements.

Replacement Process for Adults

Adults (18+) can replace a card quickly, often online. Here’s how:

Step 1: Check Eligibility

For online replacement via My SSA, you must:

  • Be 18 or older.
  • Have a U.S. mailing address (no P.O. boxes).
  • Hold a state-issued ID from an eligible state (most qualify—check here).

Step 2: Apply Online or Offline

Online: Log into My SSA, verify your ID, and submit. No paper forms needed.
Mail/In-Person: Fill out Form SS-5 (download here), then mail it or visit an office with documents.

Step 3: Receive Your Card

New card arrives in 10-14 business days. Call 1-800-772-1213 (TTY 1-800-325-0778) if it’s late—track it via My SSA.

Example: John, 35, loses his card in March 2025. He uses My SSA with his Texas driver’s license, submits online, and gets his replacement in 12 days.

Replacement Process for Children

For kids under 18, parents or guardians apply—online isn’t available. Here’s the process:

  1. Gather Documents: See the list below—child’s ID and citizenship proof are key.
  2. Complete Form SS-5: Download at SSA.gov or get it at an office.
  3. Submit: Mail to your local SSA office or visit in person (appointment recommended—see below).
  4. Wait: Card arrives in 10-14 business days.

Example: Maria replaces her 10-year-old’s card in April 2025. She mails Form SS-5 with his birth certificate, school ID, and her passport, receiving it in 11 days.

Documents Needed for Replacement

Originals or certified copies only—no photocopies or notarized docs. Here’s a detailed breakdown:

Applicant Identity Citizenship Optional (Name Change)
Adult U.S. driver’s license, state ID, or passport U.S. birth certificate or passport (if not online) Marriage certificate, divorce decree, court order
Child School ID, medical record, adoption decree (birth cert. not enough) U.S. birth certificate or passport Same as adult if applicable
Parent/Guardian Driver’s license, state ID, or passport Not required N/A

Tip: Lost all IDs? Contact your state DMV first, then use an alternate like a W-2 or Medicare card (full list at SSA.gov).

2025 Appointment Updates

Starting January 6, 2025, the SSA strongly encourages appointments for in-person visits due to high demand. Walk-ins are still allowed but expect delays—up to 2 hours in busy offices. Schedule via:

  • Phone: Call your local office (Mon–Fri, 8 AM–7 PM).
  • Online: Start at My SSA and self-schedule if prompted.

Example: Tom, 40, can’t apply online. He books an appointment for March 15, 2025, at his local office, avoiding a 90-minute walk-in wait.

Costs and Limits

Cost: Free—no fees ever. Beware of third-party sites charging $30-$50; use SSA.gov only.
Limits: 3 replacements per year, 10 lifetime (emergency exceptions possible—call SSA).

Troubleshooting Common Issues

  • Card Delayed: After 14 days, check status at 1-800-772-1213 or My SSA. Lost mail? Request a reissue.
  • Online Denied: Your state/ID might not qualify—use mail or in-person instead.
  • Name Mismatch: Update your name first with SSA (e.g., post-marriage) before replacing.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Can I replace my card if I’m not a U.S. citizen?

Yes, if you’re a legal resident (e.g., green card holder). Include your immigration docs with Form SS-5.

How long does it take in 2025?

10-14 business days from approval. Expedite isn’t available—plan ahead for jobs or benefits.

Do I need a card for my newborn?

No—apply for an SSN at birth via the hospital. Replace only if lost later.

What if I don’t have a My SSA account?

Create one at SSA.gov or use mail/in-person options.

Protect Yourself from Fraud

Scammers may offer fake replacement services in 2025. Red flags: fees, unsolicited calls, or non-SSA URLs. Report to our Fraud Guide or SSA OIG at 1-800-269-0271.
