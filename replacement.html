Social Security Card Replacement in 2025

Lost your Social Security card? Don’t worry—replacing it in 2025 is straightforward if you follow these steps. Whether online or by mail, we’ve got you covered with the latest process.

Replacement Process for Adults

You can replace your card for free (limit 3 per year, 10 lifetime) if it’s lost, stolen, or damaged. Here’s how:

Step 1: Verify Eligibility

Online replacement via My SSA requires: 18+, U.S. address, no name/SSN changes. Otherwise, use mail.

Step 2: Apply Online or Mail

Online: Log into My SSA. Mail: Submit Form SS-5 with docs.

Step 3: Receive Card

Expect your new card in 10-14 business days. No tracking—contact SSA if delayed past 3 weeks.

Examples of Replacement Scenarios

Jane: Online Replacement

Jane, 35, lost her card. She logs into My SSA, verifies her ID with a driver’s license, and gets her card in 12 days.

Mike: Mail Replacement

Mike, 50, changed his name recently. He mails Form SS-5 with his marriage certificate and passport—card arrives in 14 days.

Susan: Lost Everything

Susan’s wallet was stolen. She calls SSA, uses a school ID and birth certificate, and mails Form SS-5—card takes 16 days.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Can I replace my card online?

Yes, if 18+ with a My SSA account, U.S. address, and no SSN/name changes. Start at SSA.gov.

How long does it take?

10-14 business days. Expedite not available—plan ahead.

What if I need proof sooner?

Request a benefit verification letter via My SSA—it’s instant and often accepted.
