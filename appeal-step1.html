Step 1: Receive Denial Notice

When the Social Security Administration (SSA) denies your claim for benefits (e.g., SSI, SSDI, or retirement), they will send you an official denial notice in the mail. This letter explains why your application was denied and outlines your appeal rights.

What to Do:

  • Read the letter carefully to understand the reason for denial.
  • Note the deadline for filing an appeal (typically 60 days from the date you receive the notice).
  • Gather any additional evidence (e.g., medical records, work history) to support your case.
