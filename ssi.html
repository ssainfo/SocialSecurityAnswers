SSI Eligibility & Benefits in 2025

Supplemental Security Income (SSI) is a federal program that pays monthly cash to people with little or no income and resources who are disabled, blind, aged (65+), or certain visa holders (e.g., refugees). Unlike SSDI, SSI is needs-based—no work history required. In 2025, it supports millions with basics like food, clothing, and shelter. This guide covers eligibility, benefits, and how to apply, with examples.

What is SSI?

SSI helps those who:

  • Disabled: Adults/kids with conditions lasting 12+ months or fatal.
  • Blind: Vision 20/200 or less (better eye with glasses) or narrow field.
  • Aged: 65 or older.
  • Visa Holders: Refugees, asylees, etc., with specific statuses.

Max Payment (2025): $943/individual, $1,415/couple (after 2.5% COLA)—reduced by other income.

Example: John, 70, gets $200 pension—SSI adds $743 to reach $943.

Who Can Get SSI?

Eligibility depends on income, resources, and status:

CategoryIncome LimitResource Limit
Individual$2,000/month (earned)$2,000

Resources: Cash, extra cars count—home, one car don’t.

Residency: U.S., D.C., or Northern Mariana Islands.

Citizenship: U.S. citizens or qualified aliens (e.g., refugees < 7 years in U.S.).

SSI Benefits by Group

Aged (65+)

Benefit: Up to $943/month if low income.

Example: Maria, 67, has $800 pension, $1,000 savings—gets $143 SSI.

Adults (Disabled/Blind)

Benefit: $943/month if no income.

Example: Sarah, 35, arthritis, no job, $500 savings—gets $943.

Children (Disabled/Blind)

Benefit: Up to $943, adjusted by parents’ income.

Example: Liam, 10, autism, parents earn $2,000—gets $700.

Visa Holders

Benefit: Same as above if refugee/asylee < 7 years.

Example: Ahmed, 40, refugee, blind—gets $943.

How to Apply for SSI

Free process via the SSA:

Step 1: Check Eligibility

Confirm income/resources at SSI Eligibility.

Step 2: Gather Info

SSN, birth certificate, pay stubs, bank statements, medical records (if disabled).

Step 3: Apply

Call 1-800-772-1213 (TTY 1-800-325-0778) for appointment. Aged (65+) can start at SSA.gov. Kids need phone/in-person.

Forms:

  • SSA-8000-BK: Main app (adults/aged).
  • SSA-3820-BK: Child disability report.
  • SSA-3368-BK: Adult disability report.

Tip: Forms filled with SSA rep—preview at SSA Forms.

After You Apply

Processing: 3-5 months. Payments start next month if approved. Report changes (income, address) at SSI Reporting.

Example: Sue applies Jan 2025, approved April—first check May ($943).

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Can I get SSI and SSDI?

Yes—SSI tops up if SSDI is low.

What if I’m a refugee?

Eligible < 7 years—show visa status.

How long to get approved?

3-5 months—faster for aged online.

Get Started

Apply at SSA.gov or call 1-800-772-1213.
