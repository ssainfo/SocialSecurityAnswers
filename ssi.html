What is Supplemental Security Income (SSI)?

Supplemental Security Income (SSI) is a federal program that pays monthly cash to people with little or no income and resources who are:

  • Disabled (adults or children with a condition lasting 12+ months or expected to result in death).
  • Blind (vision 20/200 or less in the better eye with glasses, or a very narrow field of vision).
  • Aged (65 or older).

SSI is needs-based, meaning it helps those who can’t afford basics like food, clothing, and shelter. It’s different from Social Security benefits, which depend on work history. You don’t need to have worked to get SSI!

Max Payment (2025): Up to $943/month for an individual, $1,450 for a couple (reduced by other income).

Who Can Get SSI?

You might qualify if you:

  • Have income below $2,019/month from work (higher for couples or parents applying for kids).
  • Own less than $2,000 in resources ($3,000 for couples) — things like cash, bank accounts, or extra cars count, but your home and one car usually don’t.
  • Are a U.S. citizen or certain non-citizens (e.g., refugees).
  • Live in the U.S., D.C., or Northern Mariana Islands.

Note: Kids under 18 can get SSI if their disability seriously limits daily activities. Parents’ income may affect their payment.

Examples of Who Qualifies

Adult with Disability

Example: Sarah, 35, can’t work due to severe arthritis. She has no income and $500 in savings. She gets $943/month SSI.

Child with Disability

Example: Liam, 10, has autism that limits his ability to communicate and play. His parents earn little, so he gets $700/month SSI.

Aged (65+)

Example: Maria, 67, lives alone with $800/month from a pension and $1,000 in savings. Her SSI tops her up to $943/month.

How to Apply for SSI

Applying is free and starts with the Social Security Administration (SSA). Here’s how:

Step 1: Check Eligibility

Make sure your income and resources are low enough. See limits at SSI Eligibility.

Step 2: Gather Info

You’ll need your SSN, birth certificate, income proof (e.g., pay stubs), and resource details (e.g., bank statements).

Step 3: Apply

Call 1-800-772-1213 (TTY 1-800-325-0778) to schedule an appointment. Adults 65+ can start online at SSA.gov. Kids need a phone or in-person application.

Forms Needed:

  • SSA-8000-BK: Application for SSI (main form for adults and aged) - completed during your appointment.
  • SSA-3820-BK: Disability Report for Children - needed for kids under 18.
  • SSA-3368-BK: Disability Report for Adults - if applying due to disability.

Tip: You can’t download blank forms—they’re filled out with an SSA rep. Visit SSA Forms for previews.

After You Apply

It takes 3-5 months to process. If approved, payments start the month after you apply. You must report changes (e.g., income, living situation) monthly at SSI Reporting.
