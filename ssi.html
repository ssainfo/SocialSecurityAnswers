SSI Eligibility & Benefits in 2025

Supplemental Security Income (SSI) is a federal program that pays monthly cash to people with little or no income and resources who are disabled, blind, aged (65+), or certain visa holders (e.g., refugees). Unlike SSDI, SSI is needs-based—no work history required.

Max SSI in 2025: $943/month—find out if you qualify!

What is SSI?

SSI helps those who:

  • Disabled: Adults/kids with conditions lasting 12+ months or fatal.
  • Blind: Vision 20/200 or less (better eye with glasses) or narrow field.
  • Aged: 65 or older.
  • Visa Holders: Refugees, asylees, etc., with specific statuses.

SSI Benefits by Group

Aged (65+)

Benefit: Up to $943/month if low income.

Example: Maria, 67, has $800 pension—gets $143 SSI.

Adults (Disabled/Blind)

Benefit: $943/month if no income.

Example: Sarah, 35, arthritis—gets $943.

Children (Disabled/Blind)

Benefit: Up to $943, adjusted by parents’ income.

Example: Liam, 10, autism—gets $700.

Visa Holders

Benefit: Same as above if refugee/asylee < 7 years.

Example: Ahmed, 40, refugee—gets $943.

How to Apply

Free process via the SSA—call 1-800-772-1213 or start online (65+) at SSA.gov.

After You Apply

Processing takes 3-5 months. Payments start next month if approved.
