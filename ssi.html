Your 2025 SSI Guide

Everything you need to know about Supplemental Security Income—benefits, eligibility, and how to apply.

What is SSI?

Supplemental Security Income (SSI) is a federal program providing monthly cash payments to people with limited income and resources who are disabled, blind, or aged 65 or older. Unlike Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI), which requires work credits, SSI is needs-based, supporting those who may never have worked or who earn very little. In 2025, SSI helps over 7 million Americans, including adults and children, with payments adjusted for inflation. For 2025, the maximum federal benefit rate is $967 for individuals and $1,450 for couples. Learn more at SSA.gov.

SSI isn’t just for adults—children with disabilities or blindness can qualify too, and the rules change when they turn 18. Plus, certain income and resources don’t count toward eligibility, making it more accessible than you might think. Let’s dive into who qualifies and how it works.

Key Fact: SSI max payments in 2025 are $967 for individuals and $1,450 for couples, with states often adding supplements.

Who Qualifies for SSI?

Eligibility depends on three main factors: category, income, and resources. Here’s how it breaks down:

1. Category

  • Disabled: Adults or children with a physical/mental condition lasting 12+ months or expected to be fatal, severely limiting daily activities (e.g., working, walking). See disability rules.
  • Blind: Vision 20/200 or worse in the better eye with glasses, or a visual field of 20 degrees or less.
  • Aged: 65 or older, no disability required.
  • Visa Holders: Certain immigrants (e.g., refugees, asylees) within 7 years of status, if they meet disability/blindness criteria.

2. Income Limits

Your "countable income" must be below $967 (individual) or $1,450 (couple) monthly in 2025. SSA excludes some income before counting—more on that below.

Type2025 Limit (Monthly)Example
Individual$967$500 pension → $467 SSI
Couple$1,450$800 combined → $650 SSI

3. Resource Limits

Countable assets (excluding your home, one car, and some basics) must be under:

  • Individual: $2,000
  • Couple: $3,000

Check eligibility details at SSA.gov.

Income and Resource Exclusions

Not all income or assets count toward SSI limits. Here’s what’s excluded:

Income Exclusions

  • $20 General Exclusion: First $20 of any income (earned or unearned) monthly.
  • $65 Earned Income Exclusion: First $65 of wages, plus $1 reduction per $2 after that.
  • Student Earned Income: Up to $2,350/month ($9,460/year) for students under 22.
  • Food/Shelter Aid: As of 2025, food isn’t counted as in-kind support. See income rules.

Resource Exclusions

  • Your primary home and land it’s on.
  • One vehicle used for transportation.
  • Household goods and personal items (e.g., rings).
  • Burial funds up to $1,500 per person.
  • ABLE accounts up to $100,000 for disability expenses.

Explore resource exclusions at SSA.gov.

SSI Payments in 2025

Payments depend on income and living arrangements. Maximum federal rates are:

RecipientMax Monthly Payment
Individual$967
Couple$1,450
Individual in Another’s Household$645

State Supplements: Many states add to federal SSI (e.g., California adds ~$200). Check your state at SSA.gov.

How SSI Adjusts for Income

SSI reduces payments based on countable income:

  • Unearned (e.g., pensions): $1 reduction per $1 after $20 exclusion.
  • Earned (e.g., wages): $1 reduction per $2 after $85 exclusion.

Maria, 67, Retired

Maria lives alone with a $400 pension. After the $20 exclusion, her countable income is $380. SSI pays $587 ($967 - $380) monthly.

Tom, 35, Disabled Worker

Tom earns $205 monthly. After $85 exclusion, $120 is countable; half ($60) reduces his SSI: $967 - $60 = $907/month.

Application Process

Applying is free and takes 3-5 months:

  1. Gather Docs: ID, income proof, medical records (if disabled).
  2. Apply: Call 1-800-772-1213 or online (65+ only) at SSA.gov.
  3. Interview: SSA schedules a review.
  4. Decision: Payments start the next month if approved.

Real-Life Scenarios

Sarah, 12, Blind Child

Sarah’s parents earn $2,000/month. SSA deems $300 as her income, reducing her SSI to $667 ($967 - $300). She also gets Medicaid.

Jake, 18, Filing at Age 18

Jake, disabled, turns 18. Previously, his parents’ income was deemed ($500/month). Now independent with no income, he gets $967/month.

Ahmed, 40, Refugee

Ahmed, disabled, arrived in 2023 with no income and $1,000 savings. He receives $967/month until 2030 (7-year limit).

Lisa and Mark, Disabled Couple

Lisa and Mark have $600 combined pension. After $20 exclusion, $580 is countable. SSI pays $870 ($1,450 - $580) monthly.

Living Arrangements Impact

Your situation adjusts SSI:

  • Own Home: Full $967 (no income).
  • With Others: $645 if they provide food/shelter.
  • Institution: $30 if Medicaid pays.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I work and get SSI?

Yes! $400 wages → $853 SSI after exclusions. Learn more.

Does SSI affect other benefits?

Yes, often qualifies you for Medicaid, SNAP, and housing aid.
