Social Security General

The fastest and easiest way is online at ssa.gov/applyforbenefits. Over 70% of retirement applications are now submitted online. You can also call 1-800-772-1213 or visit a local office. Start your application up to 4 months before you want payments to begin. Tip: Create a my Social Security account first to pre-fill your information.

The official 2026 Cost-of-Living Adjustment is 2.8%. Benefits increased in January 2026. The average retirement check rose by about $56 to $2,071/month. Full history and details: ssa.gov/cola.

Instantly online through your my Social Security account. It is accepted for loans, housing, jobs, and more. Over 10 million letters are issued yearly. You can also request by phone or mail.

Yes — within the first 12 months of receiving retirement benefits, you can withdraw the application and repay everything received (including Medicare premiums). After 12 months, withdrawal is no longer allowed, but you can voluntarily suspend benefits after reaching Full Retirement Age (FRA). Details: ssa.gov/benefits/retirement/planner.

The Old-Age and Survivors Insurance Trust Fund is projected to pay full benefits until 2035, then about 77–80% without changes. Congress has always acted to protect benefits. Social Security has never missed a payment in 90+ years. Latest projections: ssa.gov/oact/TR.

Retirement Benefits

FRA is 66 to 67 depending on your birth year: 66 for those born 1943–1954, gradually increasing to 67 for those born 1960 or later. Use the official calculator: ssa.gov. Delaying past FRA to age 70 adds 8% per year — up to 32% more!

It depends on your health, savings, and life expectancy. Claiming at 62 reduces your benefit by about 30%. Waiting until 70 can increase it by 24–32%. The average break-even age is around 78–80. If you expect to live past 82, delaying usually pays off more. Use SSA’s calculator: ssa.gov.

Yes! Before FRA the 2026 earnings limit is $24,480 ($1 withheld for every $2 over). In the year you reach FRA the limit is $65,160. No limit after FRA. Withheld amounts are later credited back as a higher monthly benefit. Full rules: ssa.gov.

$4,152/month at Full Retirement Age for someone who paid maximum taxes for 35 years. At age 70 it can reach up to $5,181/month. Average benefit after the 2.8% COLA is $2,071/month. Check your personalized estimate: my Social Security.

Medicare

Your Initial Enrollment Period is 7 months around your 65th birthday. Missing it triggers a permanent 10% per year late-enrollment penalty on Part B. If you have employer coverage from a company with 20+ employees, you can delay Part B penalty-free. Full details: medicare.gov/sign-up.

Standard monthly premium is $185. High earners pay Income-Related Monthly Adjustment Amount (IRMAA) surcharges. Annual deductible is $257. Full IRMAA chart and costs: medicare.gov.

Under the Inflation Reduction Act, out-of-pocket prescription drug costs are capped at $2,000 per year starting 2025 and continuing in 2026. No more catastrophic phase — huge savings for people on expensive medications. Learn more: medicare.gov.

Medicare Advantage (Part C) often has $0 premium and extras like dental/vision/hearing but uses provider networks and prior authorizations. Original Medicare + Medigap offers more provider choice and predictable costs but higher premiums. Over 50% of beneficiaries chose Advantage in 2026. Compare plans: medicare.gov/plan-compare.

Part A covers inpatient hospital stays, skilled nursing facility care, hospice, and some home health. The 2026 deductible is $1,676 per benefit period. Most people pay $0 premium for Part A if they have enough work credits. Details: medicare.gov.

Medigap (Medicare Supplement Insurance) helps pay out-of-pocket costs not covered by Original Medicare (deductibles, copays, coinsurance). It is only available with Original Medicare, not Advantage. Best time to buy is during your 6-month Medigap Open Enrollment Period. Compare plans: medicare.gov.

Log into your my Social Security account or call 1-800-MEDICARE. You can print a temporary card or request a new one by mail. Never pay anyone who offers to replace it for you. Official replacement: ssa.gov/medicarecard.

Medicaid

Eligibility varies by state. In expansion states, adults qualify up to ~138% FPL (~$21,500 single). Children and pregnant women often qualify at higher levels. SSI recipients are usually automatically eligible. Check your state: medicaid.gov.

Yes — nursing homes, home health aides, and personal care services. This is one of Medicaid’s largest expense categories. Many families spend down assets to qualify. Unlike Medicare, it covers long-term custodial care.

Yes — called “dual eligible.” Medicaid often pays Medicare premiums, deductibles, and copays. The Qualified Medicare Beneficiary (QMB) program is the most common dual-eligible pathway.

Disability (SSDI)

You must have a severe disability expected to last at least 12 months or result in death, plus enough work credits (usually 40 total, 20 recent). Average initial decision time is 3–6 months. ~60% of initial claims are denied — appeals often succeed. Apply online: ssa.gov/applyfordisability.

$1,630/month after the 2.8% COLA. Maximum is $4,152 at FRA. Payments are based on your lifetime earnings. Check your estimate: my Social Security.

Yes. You get a 9-month Trial Work Period (earnings over $1,210/month count). Then a 36-month Extended Period where benefits continue unless you earn over Substantial Gainful Activity ($1,690/month non-blind in 2026). Report all work immediately: ssa.gov/work.

Apply online at ssa.gov/applyfordisability, by phone, or at a local office. You will need your Social Security number, birth certificate, medical records, and work history. SSA may request additional information during the process.

You have 60 days to request reconsideration, then a hearing before an Administrative Law Judge. Over half of hearings result in approval. Many people hire a representative (no fee unless you win). Appeal steps: ssa.gov/appeals.

There is a 5-month waiting period after the onset of disability before SSDI payments begin. There is no waiting period if you have ALS or if you are already receiving SSDI and become disabled again.

Supplemental Security Income (SSI)

Federal maximum is $943/month for an individual and $1,415/month for a couple. Many states add supplements. Payments are reduced by countable income and living arrangements. Full rules: ssa.gov/ssi.

SSI is needs-based (low income/assets), requires no work history, and usually includes automatic Medicaid. SSDI is a work-based insurance program that requires credits and provides Medicare after 24 months. ssa.gov/ssi and ssa.gov/ssdi.

Yes. The first $85 of earnings plus half of the remainder are excluded. Example: $400 wages → $157.50 countable → SSI reduced by $157.50. Always report work immediately: ssa.gov/ssi.

Survivor Benefits

Widows/widowers (age 60+, or 50+ if disabled), children under 18 (or 19 if in school), disabled adult children, and dependent parents age 62+. The deceased worker must have earned enough credits. Apply at ssa.gov/survivors.

A widow(er) at FRA receives 100% of the deceased’s benefit. At age 60 it is 71.5–99%. Children receive 75%. A family maximum applies. Example: deceased received $2,000 → widow at FRA gets $2,000, child gets $1,500.

Taxes on Benefits

Up to 85% may be taxable depending on combined income. Thresholds: $25,000 single / $32,000 joint for 50%; $34,000 / $44,000 for 85%. A new $6,000 senior deduction helps many. IRS Publication 915: irs.gov.

Request voluntary withholding (7–22%) with Form W-4V and submit it to SSA. Or make quarterly estimated payments. Most states do not tax benefits.

Social Security Card Replacement

Up to 3 times per year and 10 times lifetime (exceptions for name changes, work authorization updates, or compelling reasons do not count). It is always free. Official limits: ssa.gov/ssnumber.

Most non-citizens must verify identity in person with original DHS/USCIS documents. Start online to see if an appointment is required: ssa.gov/ssnumber.

If you are a U.S. citizen with a my Social Security account and no recent name change, you can request a replacement card online at ssa.gov/ssnumber. Most people receive the new card in 10–14 days.

You need proof of identity (driver’s license, passport) and proof of the name change (marriage certificate, divorce decree, or court order). Name-change replacements do NOT count toward the 3-per-year or 10-lifetime limits. Apply online or in person: ssa.gov/ssnumber.

You do not need the physical card for most purposes (jobs, banks, etc.). If you need a replacement, apply online or in person. Never pay anyone who offers to replace it for a fee. Protect your number: ssa.gov/ssnumber.

Fraud & Scams

No. SSA never threatens arrest, demands immediate payment via gift cards, crypto, or wire transfers. Hang up and report the call to oig.ssa.gov/report.

Shelter & Housing Assistance

Call 2-1-1 (24/7) or use HUD’s Find Shelter tool: hud.gov/find_shelter. Red Cross shelters are available during disasters — no ID required.

A HUD voucher program that helps low-income families pay rent (you pay roughly 30% of income). Waitlists are long in many areas — apply through your local Public Housing Agency (PHA).
