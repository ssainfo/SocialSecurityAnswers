Frequently Asked Questions

Social Security FAQs

How do I apply for Social Security benefits?
Apply online at SSA.gov, call 1-800-772-1213, or visit a local office. Start 4 months before you want benefits.
What is my Full Retirement Age (FRA)?
FRA is 66-67, depending on your birth year (e.g., 67 for 1960+). Check SSA.gov.
Can I work and collect Social Security?
Yes, but before FRA, earnings over $22,320 (2025) reduce benefits ($1 per $2 over). No limit after FRA.
How do I replace my Social Security card?
Use My SSA online if 18+ with a U.S. address, or mail Form SS-5 with ID. Details.

Medicare FAQs

When should I sign up for Medicare?
Sign up during your Initial Enrollment Period (3 months before to 3 months after your 65th birthday) to avoid penalties. Medicare.gov.
What does Medicare Part A cover?
Hospital stays, skilled nursing, hospice, and some home health. Free if you worked 10+ years.
Do I need Part B if I’m still working?
Not if you have employer coverage (20+ employees). Enroll later without penalty via a Special Enrollment Period.
What’s Extra Help?
A program to lower Part D drug costs for low-income folks. Apply at SSA.gov.

Medicaid FAQs

Who qualifies for Medicaid?
Low-income individuals, families, pregnant women, elderly, and disabled. Rules vary by state. Medicaid.gov.
Does Medicaid cover long-term care?
Yes, unlike Medicare, it covers nursing homes and home care if you qualify financially.
Can I have Medicare and Medicaid?
Yes, if eligible. Medicaid may pay Medicare premiums and copays. Check your state’s rules.
How do I apply for Medicaid?
Apply through your state’s Medicaid agency or HealthCare.gov. Contact info.

Housing FAQs

What is HUD housing assistance?
HUD offers vouchers, public housing, and subsidized apartments for low-income households. HUD.gov.
Who qualifies for Section 8?
Income below 50% of area median, U.S. citizens or eligible immigrants. Apply via your local Public Housing Agency (PHA).
How do I find HUD housing?
Contact your local PHA or use HUD’s online locator. PHA Contacts.
Does HUD help with home repairs?
Yes, via programs like Title I loans or local grants. Check with your PHA or HUD.gov.
