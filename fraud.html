Social Security Fraud Protection Guide

If you suspect fraud related to your Social Security benefits, identity, or number, take action quickly. Here’s how to report it, protect yourself, and prevent future issues in 2025.

Report Fraud to the SSA

If you’re experiencing Social Security fraud—such as someone misusing your benefits, impersonating SSA staff, or using your SSN—report it to the SSA Office of the Inspector General (OIG):

  • Online: Submit a report at oig.ssa.gov/report.
  • Phone: Call the Fraud Hotline at 1-800-269-0271 (10 AM–4 PM EST, Mon–Fri, excluding holidays).
  • Mail: Send details to SSA Fraud Hotline, P.O. Box 17785, Baltimore, MD 21235.

What to Include: Provide who, what, when, where, how, and why details about the suspect or incident. You can stay anonymous, but giving contact info helps if more details are needed.

Note: The OIG can’t share investigation updates due to federal privacy rules.

Protect Your Credit with the Major Bureaus

If your SSN is compromised, contact the three major credit bureaus to add a fraud alert or freeze your credit:

  • Equifax Fraud Alert - Free, lasts 1 year, renewable.
  • Experian Fraud Alert - Alerts creditors to verify your identity.
  • TransUnion Fraud Alert - Contact one bureau; they notify the others.

Next Step: Consider a credit freeze for stronger protection—it blocks access to your credit report until you lift it.

Other Actions Based on SSA Policy

Follow these SSA-recommended steps if fraud occurs:

  • Identity Theft: If someone uses your SSN for credit or services, report it to the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) at IdentityTheft.gov.
  • Direct Deposit Fraud: Add a Direct Deposit Fraud Prevention Block via your local SSA office to stop unauthorized changes (call 1-800-772-1213).
  • Payee Misuse: If a representative payee misuses your benefits, report it to the SSA immediately at 1-800-772-1213.
  • Scams: Hang up on suspicious calls claiming to be SSA (they won’t demand payment or personal info by phone). Report to oig.ssa.gov/scam.

Fraud Prevention Tips for 2025

Stay ahead of fraudsters with these SSA-inspired tips:

  • Guard Your SSN: Don’t carry your card; never share it publicly or via unsolicited calls/emails.
  • Monitor Accounts: Create a my Social Security account to check for suspicious activity.
  • Beware Phishing: Avoid clicking links in texts/emails claiming to be SSA—they won’t ask for info this way.
  • Check Credit: Review your credit reports yearly at AnnualCreditReport.com.
  • Report Lost Cards: If your SSN card is stolen, apply for a replacement at ssa.gov/ssnumber.
