Social Security Retirement Planning Guide

Social Security retirement benefits help replace part of your income when you stop working. Here’s a simple guide to plan your retirement:

When Can You Start Collecting Retirement Benefits?

  • Age 62: Earliest option, but your monthly benefit is reduced (e.g., 30% less if your Full Retirement Age is 67).
  • Full Retirement Age (FRA): 66-67, depending on your birth year. You get 100% of your retirement benefit.
  • Age 70: Waiting until 70 increases your benefit by 8% per year past FRA.

Example: Jane’s FRA is 67, and her full benefit is $2,000/month. At 62, she’d get ~$1,400. At 70, she’d get ~$2,480.

How Much Will You Get from Social Security?

Your retirement benefit is based on your 35 highest-earning years. The max in 2025 at FRA is $3,822/month.

  • Estimate It: Use My SSA or the SSA calculator. Try it.
  • Work Credits: Need 40 credits (10 years of work). In 2025, $1,730 in earnings = 1 credit.

Example: Tom worked 35 years, averaging $50,000/year. His benefit at FRA (67) might be ~$1,800/month. If he only worked 20 years, it’d be less.

Working While Collecting Retirement Benefits

Before FRA, there’s an earnings limit ($22,320 in 2025). Over that, $1 is deducted for every $2 earned. No limit after FRA.

Example: Mike, 63, gets $1,500/month and earns $30,000/year. He’s $7,680 over the limit, so $3,840 ($7,680 ÷ 2) is withheld yearly—about $320/month less until FRA.

How to Apply for Retirement Benefits

  • Online: Easiest at My SSA, 4 months before you want benefits. Apply now.
  • Phone/In-Person: Call 1-800-772-1213 or visit an office.

Tip: Start 3 months early—it can take time to process your retirement application!

Other Social Security Retirement Benefits

  • Spousal Benefits: Up to 50% of your spouse’s FRA benefit if higher than yours.
  • Divorced Benefits: If married 10+ years, unmarried now, you can claim on your ex’s record at 62+.

Example: Lisa’s FRA benefit is $1,000, but her husband’s is $2,200. She can get $1,100 (50% of his) instead.
