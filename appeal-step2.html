Step 2: File Reconsideration

Request a reconsideration if you disagree with the initial denial. This step involves a fresh review by a different SSA staff member. It applies to disability, Medicare, and non-medical appeals, and must be filed within 60 days.

Appeal Types Covered

  • Disability (SSDI/SSI): Reassess medical or vocational eligibility.
  • Medicare: Challenge denied services or Part A/B/D decisions.
  • Non-Medical: Dispute overpayments, income calculations, or eligibility.

How to File

  • Online: Use the SSA’s portal for all appeal types.
  • Form SSA-561: For disability and non-medical appeals (mail or in-person).
  • Form SSA-44: For Medicare Part B premium disputes.
  • Submit new evidence (e.g., doctor’s letters, payment records).

Example Scenarios

Disability: Jane submits Form SSA-561 with new MRI results showing her condition worsened.

Medicare: John files online to reconsider his wheelchair denial, attaching a doctor’s note.

Non-Medical: Mike uses SSA-561 to contest an overpayment, proving he reported income correctly.
