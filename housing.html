Housing Assistance Guide

HUD helps low-income families, elderly, and disabled individuals afford housing in 2025. Here’s how:

Section 8 Vouchers

Pays part of your rent based on income (30% typically). Apply through your local PHA. Waitlists may apply.

Example: Maria earns $20,000/year in an area with a $50,000 median income. Section 8 covers $600 of her $800 monthly rent.

Public Housing

Affordable apartments or homes managed by PHAs. Income-based rent, often for seniors or those on disability.

Example: John, a retiree on a fixed income, pays $300/month for a PHA apartment instead of $1,000 market rate.

Home Repairs & Loans

Title I loans or local grants help fix homes. Contact your PHA or HUD office.

Example: Lisa gets a $5,000 Title I loan to repair her roof, repayable at low interest over 10 years.

Eligibility

  • Income: Below 50-80% of area median (varies).
  • Status: U.S. citizens or eligible immigrants.

How to Apply

Find your local PHA via HUD’s PHA Locator. Apply directly.
