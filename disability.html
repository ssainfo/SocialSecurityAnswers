Your 2025 SSDI Guide

Master Social Security Disability Insurance—eligibility, application, and benefits for 2025.

What is SSDI?

Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI) provides monthly benefits to workers unable to work due to a severe, long-term disability. In 2025, it supports millions based on their work history—not financial need like SSI. Average benefits are $1,537/month, with a 2.5% COLA boost. Learn more at SSA.gov.

Key Fact: SSDI requires work credits—unlike SSI’s need-based approach.

Who’s Eligible for SSDI?

You qualify if you:

  • Have a disability lasting 1+ years or fatal.
  • Can’t do substantial gainful activity (SGA)—$1,550/month in 2025 ($2,590 if blind).
  • Earned ~40 work credits, 20 in the last 10 years ($1,730 = 1 credit in 2025).
  • Are under Full Retirement Age (67 for most in 2025).

Check eligibility at SSA.gov.

The SSDI Application Process

SSDI Application Steps

Applying involves 5 steps—60% of claims are denied initially, so details matter:

  1. Apply: Online (SSA.gov), call 1-800-772-1213, or in-person.
  2. SSA Review: Verifies work credits (1-2 months).
  3. DDS Evaluation: State DDS assesses medical evidence (3-6 months).
  4. Decision: Approved or denied via letter.
  5. Appeals (if denied): Reconsideration, hearing, or further appeals (up to 2 years).

Tip: Start online—80% of claims do—for faster processing. Apply now.

Medical Evidence Needed

Submit:

  • Diagnosis (e.g., doctor’s reports, tests).
  • Treatment records (e.g., meds, therapy).
  • Limitations (e.g., work/daily activity impact).

Mike, 50, Back Injury

Mike submits MRI scans, pain management notes, and a doctor’s letter—approved in 4 months for $1,600/month.

SSDI Benefits in 2025

Based on earnings:

  • Average: $1,537/month.
  • Max: $3,822/month.
  • Dependents: Up to 50% extra.

5-month waiting period—benefits start month 6.

Tom, 40, Onset Delay

Onset Jan 2025—approved June, first $1,800 check in July.

Work and SSDI

Trial Work Period: 9 months of work (any earnings over $1,050/month in 2025) without losing benefits.

Lisa, 38, Trial Work

Earns $800/month in TWP—benefits continue; later $1,600 exceeds SGA, benefits pause.

Appeals Process

If denied:

  • Reconsideration: Request within 60 days (10% approval).
  • Hearing: ALJ hearing—65% approval with lawyer (1-2 years).

Appeal info at SSA.gov.

2025 Updates

  • COLA: 2.5%—$1,537 average.
  • SGA: $1,550 ($2,590 blind).

Frequently Asked Questions

How long for approval?

3-6 months; 1-2 years with appeals.

Can I work while applying?

Yes, under $1,550—but it may hurt your case.
