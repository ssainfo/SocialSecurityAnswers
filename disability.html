Social Security Disability Insurance (SSDI) 2026 – Complete Guide

Monthly benefits for workers unable to work due to severe, long-term disability. Full process, official forms, detailed appeals, Medicare timeline, free legal help & work incentives.

✓ Who Qualifies for SSDI in 2026?

Medical Condition

Must last (or be expected to last) at least 12 months or result in death.

Work Credits

Usually 40 total (20 in last 10 years). $1,890 earnings = 1 credit in 2026.

Under Full Retirement Age

Must be under FRA (benefits convert to retirement at FRA).

Below SGA

Earnings under $1,690/month (non-blind) or $2,830/month (blind).

SSDI Appeals Process – What to Do If Denied (2026)

Only ~40% approved initially. You have 60 days to appeal – do NOT reapply.

Reconsideration

New reviewer looks at your file (10–15% approval).

Hearing

Administrative Law Judge (ALJ) – 45–65% approval with lawyer (12–18 months wait).

Appeals Council

Review of ALJ decision.

Federal Court

Last resort.

Recommendation: Get free or contingency-fee representation immediately after denial.

Medicare Eligibility Timeline After SSDI Approval

Month 1–24

SSDI only (no Medicare yet).

Month 25

Medicare Part A & B begin automatically.

Exceptions

ALS: Medicare from month 1. ESRD: immediate.

Pro Tip: Sign up for Medicare during your initial enrollment period to avoid late penalties.
