Social Security Disability Benefits Guide - SSDI and SSI

Social Security offers two disability benefits programs—SSDI and SSI—to help if you can’t work due to a severe condition. Here’s the basics, explained simply:

SSDI - Social Security Disability Insurance

  • Who Qualifies: Workers with enough credits (e.g., 40 total, 20 in last 10 years) unable to work for 12+ months due to a severe condition.
  • Benefit: Based on your earnings history—average $1,500/month in 2025.
  • Waiting Period: 5 months after approval before payments start.

Example: John, 45, worked 15 years and earned 40 credits. A car accident leaves him unable to work. He gets approved and receives $1,700/month after 5 months.

SSI - Supplemental Security Income

  • Who Qualifies: Low-income people (under $2,000 in resources for individuals) with a disability, blind, or 65+. No work credits needed.
  • Benefit: Up to $943/month for individuals in 2025, less if you have other income.

Example: Maria, 30, has a disability and no work history. Her savings are $1,500. She qualifies for $943/month from SSI.

How to Apply for Disability Benefits

  • Online: Fastest way at SSA.gov. Apply now.
  • Phone/In-Person: Call 1-800-772-1213 or visit an office.
  • Time: Takes 3-5 months. Apply early—back payments may cover delays.

Tip: You’ll need medical records, work history, and ID to apply for disability benefits. Start gathering them now!

Working While on Disability Benefits

Trial Work Period: Test work for 9 months (earning over $1,110/month in 2025) without losing benefits.

Ticket to Work: Free program to help you return to work. Learn more.

Example: Sarah gets $1,200/month SSDI. She tries a part-time job earning $1,500/month for 6 months. Her benefits stay during this trial.

Appealing a Disability Denial

About 2/3 of first applications are denied. Appeal within 60 days—steps include reconsideration, hearing, and more.

Example: Mike’s SSDI claim is denied. He appeals with new medical proof and wins at a hearing 8 months later.
