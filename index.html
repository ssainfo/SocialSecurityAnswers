5 Real-World Examples: Documents Required (per SSA POMS)

POMS (Program Operations Manual System) is the official SSA policy manual. The RM 00203 series details exactly which original documents prove identity, immigration status, and work authorization. Always bring original unexpired documents only.

1. U.S. Citizen – Lost SSN Card

POMS RM 00203.020 & RM 00203.030

  • Valid U.S. driver’s license or state ID
  • Passport or birth certificate (if no photo ID)

One photo ID that also proves U.S. citizenship is often enough.

2. H-1B Specialty Occupation Visa

POMS RM 00203.100

  • Valid passport with H-1B visa stamp
  • I-94 arrival record
  • Form I-797 approval notice
  • Labor Condition Application (LCA)

RM 00203.100 explicitly requires I-797 + LCA for H-1B work authorization.

3. F-1 Student (OPT/CPT)

POMS RM 00203.100

  • Valid passport
  • I-20 with DSO SSN endorsement
  • I-94
  • EAD card (for OPT)
  • School letter confirming SSN need

School letter is mandatory under POMS to verify authorized F-1 activity.

4. Green Card / Permanent Resident

POMS RM 00203.100

  • Green Card (Form I-551)
  • Valid passport

I-551 Green Card is primary proof of permanent work authorization.

5. EAD / Employment Authorization Holder

POMS RM 00203.100

  • Valid passport
  • Form I-766 EAD card
  • I-797 (if applicable)

I-766 EAD is definitive proof of work authorization per POMS.

⚠️ Common Mistakes to Avoid (2026)

  • Using photocopies instead of originals
  • Going to SSA before 10 days after entry (SEVIS/I-94 not updated)
  • Applying online when you are a non-citizen or first-time applicant
  • Paying third-party sites that charge fees (SSA is always free)
  • Forgetting the school/job letter for F-1 or H-1B

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I replace my SSN card online?
Yes — eligible U.S. citizens (18+, no record changes) can replace instantly via my Social Security.
What is the current replacement limit?
3 per year and 10 lifetime (exceptions for name or work authorization changes).
Do I need an appointment?
Non-citizens and first-time applicants must schedule via ICON after starting online at ssa.gov.
